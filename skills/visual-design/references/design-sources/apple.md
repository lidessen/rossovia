# Apple Human Interface Guidelines

## Use for

Adaptive content/control hierarchy, reading order, grouping, alignment,
typography, and semantic color under changing platform and accessibility
conditions.

## Decision lenses

- [Layout](https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/layout)
  relates essential information, reading order, alignment, controls, content,
  and adaptation across devices and text sizes.
- [Typography](https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/typography)
  treats type as a means to preserve legibility and communicate relative
  importance under text-size changes.
- [Color](https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/color)
  treats color as communication, continuity, state, and hierarchy across
  appearance and contrast modes.

## Transfer boundary

Recover the content, hierarchy, adaptation, and semantic-role decisions. Do not
copy Apple materials, navigation, margins, system colors, typeface, or product
surface into a non-Apple context. Platform familiarity may outweigh custom
identity only when the actual target platform establishes that condition.
