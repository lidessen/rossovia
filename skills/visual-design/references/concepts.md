# Adaptive Visual Design

## In plain words

Good design does not begin by choosing a look. It begins by asking what this
particular artifact must make perceptible to this particular audience under its
actual conditions. A visual direction is the working answer to that relation.
Style is only one visible consequence and may need to change completely in a
different context.

## Essence

1. **Situated relation:** object, audience, action, history, and constraints
   select the direction.
2. **Generative contrast:** a direction gains meaning from what it chooses and
   what it refuses, not from a list of adjectives.
3. **Coherent differentiation:** related surfaces share enough identity to feel
   like one system while retaining forms appropriate to their different jobs.
4. **Embodied evidence:** a rendered artifact reveals failures that a brief or
   token sheet cannot.
5. **Judgment cultivation:** reviewed cases can improve future judgment, but no
   successful treatment becomes universal taste by repetition alone.

## Relation, direction, system, and treatment

```text
project purpose + material history + selected source field
  -> provisional project direction

provisional project direction + object + audience + action + conditions
  -> case-specific perceptual relation
     -> case-specific direction
        -> shared system invariants
           -> treatments adapted to surfaces and states
              -> artifact-in-use observation
                 -> evidence for retaining or revising project direction
```

- The **source field** retains attributed distinctions from relevant work,
  including counterexamples and human preference evidence.
- A **project direction** is one revisable organizing proposition synthesized
  before a continuing family of work; it prevents every task from inventing a
  new taste.
- The **relation** is what the audience must be able to perceive or do.
- The **direction** organizes choices for one bounded context.
- The **system** preserves identity across related artifacts.
- A **treatment** is the visible local realization.

Promotion requires evidence. A treatment does not define the system merely
because it is attractive. A system does not become a portable direction merely
because one project adopted it.

## Two timescales

Cultivation happens infrequently: a project studies sources, compares genuine
alternatives, receives human preference evidence, and establishes a provisional
direction. Production happens repeatedly: a case inherits that direction,
adapts it to a concrete audience and function, embodies it, and sends observed
failure back to future cultivation.

Neither timescale replaces the other. Pure top-down style becomes dogma; pure
case-by-case adaptation forgets accumulated taste and makes every artifact feel
unrelated.

“Good” is relational here. A source may be exemplary for task clarity and poor
for warmth, historically durable but inaccessible in a current interface, or
personally compelling without demonstrated public usefulness. The source record
must name the dimension of merit and its evidence; admiration alone cannot make
the whole artifact authoritative.

## Fixed-style failure

A skill has become a style preset when it:

- supplies default colors, fonts, radii, imagery, grid, or motion before reading
  the object;
- turns a broad identity description into an invented token specification;
- uses the same mood vocabulary across unrelated audiences and actions;
- mistakes identical components for coherence;
- copies a reference's surface instead of recovering the decision it enabled;
- treats project-local preference as general aesthetic truth; or
- rejects a fitting artifact because it does not resemble the skill author's
  prior work.

The correction is not random variety. Return to the concrete relation, retain
hard constraints, generate meaningful contrasts, and let the treatment follow.

## Authority boundary

The skill may propose a case, design an artifact, and provide review evidence.
It cannot establish product truth, brand authority, or aesthetic acceptance.
Project sources own facts and identity; actual tools own execution capability;
the named human or reviewer owns acceptance.
